Carrier Testing

The Carrier Testing aims to identify individuals carrying a single copy of a mutated gene that, when combined with another mutated copy from a partner, can raise the risk of their offspring developing a specific genetic disorder, such as cystic fibrosis and Tay-Sachs disease.

Predictive Testing

Predictive testing evaluates a person's likelihood of developing a specific illness by analyzing their genetic composition. For instance, it can determine the risk of breast and ovarian cancer with BRCA1 and BRCA2 mutations, as well as the susceptibility to Alzheimer's disease with the APOE-e4 gene.

Diagnostic Testing

Diagnostic testing is crucial to confirm the existence of a particular genetic disorder once symptoms have manifest. For instance, prenatal testing can determine the presence of Down syndrome, while Huntington's disease testing can provide conclusive evidence for its occurrence.
